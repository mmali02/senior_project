  • Composting: We compost veggie scraps from the Dining Commons, and food waste from the 4 student kitchens in the GLC. The nutrient-rich compost is then used for growing more vegetables, and fertilizing our fruit trees.
  • Seed->Harvest->Table: Kale, tomatoes, lettuce, onions, beets, and more! We love growing food for the dining commons.
  • Apiary: The SB Beekeepers Association manages two bee drawers in our garden. The bees help increase the health of our garden by pollinating our vegetables, trees and wildflowers!
  • Biodiversity: the garden provides a great place to marvel at God's creation, and praise Him for His glorious works! It's a great place to go birdwatching. The ecology class did some surveying in the garden, and one group found 15 different species of butterflies in the garden!
